# Hardwired Methodology

**Version:** 3.1 · May 2026
**Supersedes:** v3.0 · April 2026
**Status:** Working methodology · Always in beta

**v3.1 changes:** The era evaluation, previously implicit in era-relevance and mediation-exposure dimensions, is now named explicitly and operationalised as three tests. Mediation exposure is connected to the agent-era ledger logic — direct relationships become entries in the buyer's operating context; mediated channels do not. No grades require revision under v3.1; the change is operational, not doctrinal.

---

## The three questions

Every Hardwired assessment answers three questions. Nothing else.

**1. What are the company's differences?** What does this company offer that is not commodity? Name them specifically. A difference that cannot be named is not a difference.

**2. Is the company protecting them?** Differences come under attack from three directions. External competitors with better offerings. Internal erosion as the company drifts from its own philosophy under financial, leadership, or market pressure. Era-structural threats — AI substitution, agent disintermediation, category commoditisation. The company has to defend against all three. Protection is assessed by what the company is *doing*, not what it is *saying*.

**3. Is the company capitalising where it can?** Where the era permits new revenue or expanded position, is the company capturing it? This question applies only where the company's philosophy authorises expansion. For companies whose philosophy explicitly privileges protection over expansion (Hermès, Ferrari, Costco, Berkshire), the question does not apply and the absence of capitalising is not a failure.

The grade is the honest answer to these three questions, validated by financial evidence.

---

## The role of financial performance

Financial performance is not a separate dimension. It is evidence.

If the company's structural story says protection is working, revenue trajectory and margin trend should confirm it. If the company's structural story says capitalising is happening, new revenue should be visible in segment disclosures, customer counts, or market share.

When structural story and financial evidence agree, the grade is defensible.
When they diverge, the structural story is wrong or incomplete. Re-examine.

**Specific evidence to weigh:**
- 3-year revenue trajectory (growth, flat, declining)
- Margin trend (expanding, stable, compressing)
- Capital returns trajectory
- Market cap trajectory relative to category peers
- Customer or user counts where disclosed
- Market share trends where available
- Talent retention evidence where observable

Financial performance earns its place because a company with declining revenue is either (a) failing to protect its differences, (b) protecting differences that aren't defensible, or (c) mid-investment cycle that will reverse the trend. The scorer has to specify which, with evidence.

## The strategic capacity reality

A company's capacity to act on its differences is bounded by its financial position. Nvidia can acquire whatever future it wants. BMW cannot. This matters, and the assessment has to register it.

Capacity operates through three channels:
- **Acquisition optionality.** Can the company buy the threats that appear? Can it buy the capabilities it lacks?
- **Investment sustainability.** Can the company sustain its chosen strategic investment through cyclical pressure without abandoning it?
- **Weather-the-storm capacity.** Can the company outlast era-structural compression while its integrated system adjusts?

Capacity does not substitute for the answer to the three questions. A Mag 7 company with massive capacity but no coherent philosophy is not hardwired; it is merely large. A small company with strong hardwiring but limited capacity is genuinely hardwired but strategically fragile — the grade should reflect both truths.

**The Mag 7 reality.** Seven companies currently represent a disproportionate share of total public-market capitalisation and absorb a disproportionate share of investor attention, talent, and partnership leverage. This changes what it means to be hardwired as a non-Mag-7 company. Non-Mag-7 companies should be assessed against the actual competitive set they face, which includes the reality that Mag 7 players can outspend them, outhire them, and set terms in any partnership. A non-Mag-7 company's grade carries an implicit fragility marker relative to a Mag 7 company's grade at the same letter.

## The enforcement test

Before accepting any claim that a company has strong philosophy enforcement, answer: is the philosophy actually capable of stopping a decision inside the company? Who holds that authority, and have they used it recently?

If you can name the function and a recent moment when it blocked or delayed something, the philosophy is operational.

If the authority exists on paper but has lost weight, the philosophy is drifting.

If no such authority exists, the philosophy is communications.

A philosophy that cannot stop a decision is not protecting anything.

## The era evaluation

A company's position under next-generation LLM conditions is tested at three layers. The three tests are not equivalent. A company can pass one, fail another, and have the third be inapplicable by design. The tests must be read together.

**Synthesis layer — what frontier models surface about the company under direct query.**

Tested empirically: query the major frontier models (GPT, Claude, Gemini) with a question like *"what is distinctive about [company]?"* and record what surfaces. The test asks whether the documented public record about the company — the corpus models are trained on — accurately reflects the company's structural identity. If models surface the company as something other than what the company believes itself to be, the company has a synthesis-layer problem regardless of how strong its internal philosophy is. Models are not reading press releases; they are reading the public record. Synthesis layer drifts on a thirty-day clock as the documented record evolves and as model versions update.

**Agent-callability — whether an agent acting on a buyer's intent can complete a commercial path with the company under its philosophy.**

This is the operational test. An agent attempting to act on a buyer's intent — *book a table, buy this product, schedule this service, transact this purchase* — needs the company to expose enough structured commercial reality that the action can complete. Three sub-questions:

- *What is present?* Named integrations, exposed catalogues, programmatic APIs, agent-channel partnerships, dated and verifiable.
- *What is absent?* Commercial fields not yet exposed where the philosophy would authorise them — pricing not callable, inventory not exposed, payment authority not delegable.
- *What does the philosophy authorise?* Some companies' integrated systems do not authorise agent-callability. Hermès's waitlists, Costco's membership-warehouse model, Ferrari's allocation-by-relationship are structurally non-callable by design. Their failure to be callable is not a deficiency under the framework; it is the philosophy operating correctly. Other companies' philosophies would authorise callability and the absence is structural failure — BMW's lack of agent-channel posture, most Varnished cluster companies' surface-AI announcements without the substrate underneath.

The principled-passivity rule applies here as elsewhere: a company in an era-orthogonal pool whose philosophy does not authorise agent-callability is correctly non-callable, and the framework should not penalise the absence. A company whose philosophy authorises but has not built it is in different territory.

**Agent-readability — whether the structural commitments that make the company not-commodity are documented in a form models can cite.**

Tested by naming a published, verifiable structural commitment that an agent could cite as the brand's actual difference. *Costco's 14% markup cap is documented in earnings calls going back four decades.* *Hermès's two-year apprenticeship requirement is publicly reported.* *Patagonia's 1% for the Planet commitment is structural.* These are facts, available in the documented record, that an agent doing real work for a buyer would surface as the brand's specific difference. Companies whose only "difference" is a marketing line — *"sheer driving pleasure," "think different," "just do it"* without a structural commitment behind them — fail this test even if their synthesis layer surfaces the marketing line, because the line is not actionable evidence of structural difference.

**The four hedges, restated.** The substrates document specifies that hardwiring produces four specific hedges against decision mediation. These map directly onto the agent-callability and agent-readability tests:

1. **Direct customer relationships** — the buyer talks to the company without an agent in the middle. Mediation exposure low, agent-callability question redirected. Costco membership, Apple device installed base, Amex cardmember relationship, Hermès waitlists.
2. **Category identity LLMs describe as canonical** — the company is the reference point in the corpus. Synthesis layer is permanently favourable. Hermès on luxury, Visa on payment networks, Costco on warehouse retail.
3. **Capital authority for agent-layer partnerships** — the company has the institutional standing to negotiate structural placement at the agent layer. Microsoft–OpenAI, Walmart–OpenAI, Mastercard's agent partnerships. Agent-callability strong because the company has the capital to make it so.
4. **Institutional trust signals** — multi-decade institutional reputation that weights the brand in agent outputs. JPMorgan, Mastercard, the New York Times. Synthesis layer favourable because the institutional record is dense.

A company strong on at least two of the four hedges is structurally defended at the agent layer. A company strong on none is exposed regardless of its current revenue trajectory.

**Mediation exposure as the agent-era ledger test.**

Mediation exposure was named in v3 as the second overlay alongside pool classification. v3.1 names what mediation exposure actually tests in the agent era. *Low mediation* means the company has direct relationships with its customers, which means the company can become an entry in each individual buyer's operating context — an entry in the agent's ledger when that buyer's agent is acting. *High mediation* means the customer reaches the company through retailers, search, mass advertising, or aggregators, which means the agent's ledger entry is for the mediating channel rather than for the company. Coca-Cola's brand is held in the buyer's mind; Coca-Cola's relationship is held by Walmart, Amazon, and the supermarkets. Under agent-mediated buying, the ledger entry that gets called is the retailer's, not the brand's.

This is why low-mediation companies cluster at the strongest grades in the agent era: not because of the pool they sit in, but because the buyer's agent has them in its operating context directly.

## Adversarial discipline

The default failure mode is scoring what the company *claims* rather than what it *enforces*. Apply the following checks before finalising any strong claim:

**On protecting differences:**
- Name a decision in the last 24 months that the philosophy ruled out. (Not ruled in. Ruled out.)
- Name three decisions in the last 5 years that cost the company measurable short-term money and were only defensible on philosophy grounds.
- Could the current response to era-threats have been meaningfully different under the same philosophy? If any response is justifiable, the posture is unfalsifiable and should not count as strong evidence.

**On capitalising:**
- Is the new revenue showing up in realised numbers, or is it announced, targeted, or promised?
- Captured means revenue on the income statement. Announcements are not capture.

**On leadership:**
- Has the current leader survived at least one strategic reframe not widely predicted at the time?
- Has the leader survived a crisis of their own making, not an external shock?
- Founder-CEOs whose company has not yet survived a transition cannot score at the highest tier regardless of individual performance.

**On the integrated system:**
- Would dismantling the institutional infrastructure cost the company something? If not, it is not load-bearing.
- Does the structure actively constrain decisions, or is it dormant machinery?

If the charitable answer to any of these fails, the claim does not hold at the highest tier.

## The drift-toward-charity failure mode

Named because it happens.

When scoring an interesting company with a strong articulated philosophy, the scorer can produce a charitable grade that survives a superficial read of primary sources. Under pushback, each critique gets absorbed and the grade adjusts down a notch. Three or four rounds of pushback produce a grade that is still one notch above the honest grade, because the scorer is anchoring on the initial charitable read.

**Watch for this.** If a grade is migrating consistently downward across multiple rounds of review, the scorer is probably still anchored above the honest grade. The productive move is to reset — score the company fresh under the core three questions without reference to the earlier scores.

**The BMW case (April 2026) is the canonical example.** Initial score A−. Migrated through A, A−, B−, C+ across five rounds. C+ was the honest answer. The correct response to the first round of pushback would have been to reset rather than adjust one notch.

## Grade definitions

Grades are an honest judgment of how well the company is answering the three questions, validated by financial evidence.

**A** — The company has named, defensible differences. It is actively protecting them against all three attack vectors. It is capitalising where its philosophy authorises. The financial evidence confirms the structural story. Examples: Hermès, Microsoft, Amazon, Nvidia, Costco, Eli Lilly, Thomson Reuters.

**A−** — The same pattern with one significant weakness or uncertainty. The integrated system is real and working; one component is still maturing or facing pressure the system is actively managing. Examples: Muji, Publicis, Palantir, Shopify.

**B** — The differences are real and named. Protection is working in most dimensions but has specific gaps. Capitalising is partial or uneven. Financial evidence shows stability rather than compounding. Examples: Meta, Netflix, NYT, SAP, JPMorgan (with gate 5 partial).

**B−** — Integrated system exists but is under genuine pressure. Protection is holding in some dimensions and eroding in others. Financial evidence shows the pressure. Re-scoring trigger within 12-24 months likely. Examples: LVMH, Tesla, Starbucks, McDonald's.

**C+** — The structural story exists but is hollowing out. One or more of the three questions is answered weakly. Financial evidence confirms the structural weakness. The company is recognisably hardwired but the wiring is fraying. Examples: Block, Booking, Apple (under current adversarial tests), BMW (April 2026).

**C** — Fragments of integrated system remain but incoherent. Multiple attack vectors producing visible erosion. Financial evidence shows the erosion. Examples: PayPal, American Express, Disney, Expedia.

**D or below** — The integrated system is absent or broken. The company is operating without coherent philosophy, active protection, or defensible differences. Financial evidence shows structural compression without structural response. Examples: Chegg, Gartner, H&R Block, Teleperformance, ZoomInfo, WPP, Comcast (F).

## Structural descriptors (kept from v2.1 as useful context)

**Durability flag.** Describes the temporal pattern of the hardwiring, independent of current grade.
- *Inherited* — multi-generational philosophy plus infrastructure
- *Reformulated* — inherited foundation, current leadership has substantially restated for new conditions
- *Leader-built* — current-leadership-built philosophy inside an institution that had no prior coherent foundation
- *Founder-active* — still in founding period; will become Inherited or Reformulated at transition

**Pool classification.** Describes the revenue environment, independent of current grade.
- *Era-amplified* — LLMs and agents make the category more valuable
- *Era-stable* — LLMs and agents do not materially affect the category
- *Era-threatened* — LLMs and agents compress the category
- *Era-orthogonal* — LLMs and agents do not route through the category

**Mediation exposure.** Describes how customers reach the company.
- *Low* — direct relationships, membership, physical presence, institutional trust
- *High* — mass advertising, search, retailer-mediated, aggregator-dependent
- *Mixed* — split portfolios

These descriptors inform grade interpretation but do not determine grade. A company can be Inherited and still score C. A company can be era-threatened and still score A if the integrated system is defending effectively.

## The assessment procedure

For each company:

1. **Identify the differences.** Name them specifically. If you cannot name three, the company is more commodity than it appears.

2. **Assess protection against external competitors.** Who is attacking these differences? What is the company doing about it? Is the response working, as evidenced by financials?

3. **Assess protection against internal erosion.** Is the company drifting from its own philosophy? Apply the enforcement test. Is the philosophy operational or communications?

4. **Assess protection against era-structural threats.** Which of the four LLM-era attack mechanisms apply (direct substitution, AI-accelerated competitor displacement, agent disintermediation, category commoditisation)? What is the structural defence?

5. **Assess capitalising.** Does the philosophy authorise era-expansion? If yes, is it happening? If no, the question does not apply and absence is not failure.

6. **Apply the era evaluation.** Three tests, run together: synthesis layer (what models surface), agent-callability (what the company is doing at the agent-protocol layer, what the philosophy authorises, what's absent that should be present), agent-readability (which structural commitments models can cite). Identify which of the four hedges from the substrates document the company has at least two of: direct customer relationships, category identity, capital authority for partnerships, institutional trust signals. A company with fewer than two hedges is exposed at the agent layer regardless of its current revenue trajectory.

7. **Check the evidence.** Does financial performance confirm or contradict the structural story? If contradict, re-examine.

8. **Apply strategic capacity.** Does the company have the financial capacity to sustain its strategic position? If not, the grade caps below the nine-dimension reading suggests.

9. **Assign durability flag and pool classification** as context.

10. **Grade honestly.** Use the adversarial tests. Watch for drift-toward-charity. Reset if multiple rounds of review keep pulling the grade in the same direction.

11. **Document the verification trail.** Primary sources cited. Financial evidence cited. Adversarial tests applied. Era evaluation results recorded — synthesis-layer query results dated, transaction-layer integrations named with dates, agent-readable structural commitments cited. Re-scoring triggers listed.

## Re-scoring triggers

Publicly documented so nobody has to ask.

- CEO transition within 12 months
- Major acquisition or divestiture (>5% of market cap)
- Category-structural event (AI-native entrant reaching scale, major regulatory change, breakthrough capability release)
- Financial-structural event (material revenue decline, margin compression, capital-structure change)
- Methodology version bump

## The publishable-vs-apparatus discipline

The apparatus is the source of truth. Publishable verdicts follow the apparatus. When the apparatus changes a grade, the publishable updates, even at cost to narrative consistency with earlier work.

Grades are dated and methodology-stamped. A grade produced on date X under methodology Y carries both markers.

---

## The lesson underneath the lessons

The framework is a test of whether a company has an honest internal conscience about what makes it not-commodity, and whether that conscience still has authority — expressed in deliberate decisions, institutional structure, leadership calibration, and financial outcomes.

Everything else is apparatus for catching the honest answer to that question.

When the apparatus and the honest answer diverge, trust the honest answer and update the apparatus. When they agree, you have earned the grade.
